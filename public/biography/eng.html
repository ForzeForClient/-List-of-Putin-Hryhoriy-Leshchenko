MY BIOGRAPHY

 

          I asked an online library to post my book Putin's List. The library owners offered to write a biography.

           I read a lot of biographies. The biographies are all the same.  Who people became. What they did.

          I will write who I did not become.  What I did not do.

          I was born in Ukraine.

          I was born in Central Ukraine.  Poltava region.  Two funny Ukrainian writers, Mykola Gogol and Ostap Vyshnia, were born here. I love these writers very much.

          I was born in the village of Deikalivka.

          My mother worked as a teacher. My father and his brother worked in a collective agricultural enterprise. My father worked as an engineer. My uncle worked as a storekeeper.

          There were a lot of thieves in the Soviet Union. In the Soviet Union, all enterprises belonged to the state or the collective.

          In the Soviet Union, people received small salaries. Many people stole at work. People took the property of enterprises home. Stealing from work was a common thing.

          It was a disease. It was kleptomania.  The disease of parents in the Soviet Union in the 20th century turned into the corruption of children in Ukraine in the 21st century.

          My uncle was a thief. He stole from the state and the company.

          Everyone was afraid of my uncle. Uncle burned down a neighbor's house. My uncle bought the ashes and expanded his yard. My uncle moved into a new house that was built for my parents.

          My uncle and his wife had a large household. Chickens, ducks, geese, rabbits, pigs, cows, and bulls. My uncle stole food for poultry and cattle at work.

          I was very fond of my uncle.

          My uncle loved me very much. My uncle wanted to have a son. But my uncle had only two daughters.

          My uncle wanted me to become like him.

          My uncle told my aunt to love me like her own son. My aunt refused. My uncle told me to settle the matter with my aunt. I threatened my aunt that I would burn her in the barn. My aunt fell in love with me. The villagers learned about my threats and started calling me "Fritz". During the Second World War, the Germans drove people into houses and burned them alive.

          My uncle really liked my threats to my aunt.

          My parents were afraid that I would become like my uncle.

          In 1973, my parents and I moved to the town of Zinkiv.

          My parents didn't want me to communicate with my uncle.

          I started going to the best school in town. I became an excellent student. I won competitions in Ukrainian language, literature, geography, and history.

          My mother taught me to love books. I read books all the time.

          My uncle was very greedy. My uncle raised bulls. My uncle sold bulls. My uncle did not want to invite professional bull killers. He killed the bulls himself. A bull hit my uncle and my uncle died.       My uncle was dying. My uncle called me. My uncle did not call his wife and daughters.

          My aunt and her daughters don't like me. My uncle loved me more than them.

          I do not communicate with my aunt and cousins.

          My uncle stole a lot of money from the state and enterprises. My uncle was very fond of money. Uncle had several bags of money. Uncle kept money in hiding places.

          My aunt took all the money my uncle stole to the bank. The bank went bankrupt and all the money was lost.

          My father and I often went to my uncle's grave. I cried and hugged the grave.

          I asked my uncle for forgiveness. I did not betray my uncle. I did not want to be like my uncle.

          I DID NOT BECOME A BANDIT, A THIEF, A SHADOW MILLIONAIRE.

          At school, I criticized the biggest leader of the city of Zinkiv and the district. The head of the district committed car scams, robbed businesses, and had many mistresses.  I wanted to show my children how brave I was. I'm not afraid to criticize leaders.

          Schoolchildren denounced me.

          The head's wife took revenge on me for criticizing her husband. The head's wife controlled the schools in the district. My wife came to the exam to see me. I was nervous and made a mistake at the exam.

          I DID NOT GET A GOLD MEDAL AT SCHOOL.

          In the Soviet Union, parents used to give bribes for their children to enter higher education institutions.

          My parents wanted me to become a geography teacher.  My parents became friends with the dean of the geography department at the university. My parents gave bribes to get me into the university. The dean was very greedy. My parents had no money. My father stole things and food at work. My father gave the stolen food to the dean. I used to laugh at the dean. The dean heard my jokes and refused to take me to study at the university.

          I DID NOT BECOME A GEOGRAPHY TEACHER.

          The dean agreed to take my sister to study. My sister became the dean of the university's geography department.  My relatives asked my sister to arrange for her nephews to study at the university.  My sister demanded very large bribes.  The nephews' parents had no money for bribes. My sister is an academic, she has written 200 scientific papers. My sister does not accept students for studying without bribes. When I asked my sister to help me with my business, my sister received a bribe from me. My sister is a bribe-taker. I love my sister very much.

          I DID NOT BECOME A BRIBE TAKER.

          My father had a friend. My father and his friend were born in Deikalivka and went to school together. My father's friend was an admiral. My father's friend was a big leader in the capital of the Soviet Union, Moscow. He controlled all the military naval schools in the Soviet Union.

          The admiral and I were very fond of the sea. The admiral wanted me to become an officer in the navy.  The admiral promised to arrange for me to enter the naval school in Sevastopol, Crimea. I dreamed of becoming a naval officer and sailing on a ship.

          My father had an older brother. My uncle was the head of the fire ground in Sevastopol.  My uncle did nothing at work. My uncle was a lazy man. My uncle slept at work and swam in the Black Sea. My uncle wrote fake documents about firefighting training.

          My uncle loved himself very much. For 50 years, my uncle did not lift weights over 3 kilograms.

          My uncle took me on a tour of a warship. The sailors told me about the terrible rules of life on the ship. It was very hard and boring to serve on a ship. 

          I DID NOT BECOME A NAVAL OFFICER.

          My parents met a professor at the Taras Shevchenko National University of Kyiv. This is the best higher education institution in Ukraine. The professor worked at the Faculty of Journalism.     He arranged for me to study at the university. I wanted to become a journalist.

          The professor is very similar to my uncle from Deikalivka. The professor has the same name as my uncle from Deikalivka. Charismatic. Big. Strong. Bald. Smart. Pretending to be a simpleton. Cunning. He knows how to communicate with anyone. The professor has written many books.

          I immediately fell in love with the professor. I was very happy when I talked to the professor.

          The professor wanted me to become a scientist. He wanted me to continue his work. I was very proud of the professor's offer. I was very proud. But I was not interested in the work of a scientist. 

          The professor taught us a boring, unnecessary subject. But the professor told funny stories in his lectures. Other teachers were afraid to behave like that. They said that the professor cooperated with the secret police. The professor was a secret police agent. In the Soviet Union, it was impossible to make a career without the secret police. The professor arranged for more than 100 children from villages to study at the university.

          After graduation, 10 of my classmates got jobs in the secret police. During their studies, they wrote denunciations. My classmate became a general of the secret police. When Russia's war against Ukraine began, the general fled the service.

          The professor has three sons. His sons do not want to be scientists. The eldest son steals money together with the Kyiv city utilities. The younger son is a lawyer who bribes judges.

          Ukrainians elected the middle son to the Parliament of Ukraine.  The son wanted to become a minister or president. His competitors organized a big scandal against him. The son's career was over. 

          The enemies took revenge on the professor for writing articles about Ukraine's shortcomings. 

          The professor became blind with worry.

          The professor is a holy man. I am an improved version of the professor. I think like a professor. But I do not suck up to the authorities. I do not cooperate with the secret police.

          I pray for the professor's health. The professor is over 90 years old.

          I thank God for sending the professor to me.

          The professor and his sons think and speak literary Ukrainian. Many Ukrainian politicians and celebrities think in Russian.

          Why are the professor's sons of no use to Ukraine?

          I DID NOT BECOME A UNIVERSITY PROFESSOR.

          I was studying to be a television journalist. Every summer I worked for the Kharkiv city television. I criticized those whom other journalists did not want to criticize. I was nicknamed the Television Killer. I gave gifts to the management of Kharkiv television. I was invited to work at Kharkiv TV. 

          My mother told me that the journalists of the newspaper in Zinkiv do not want to work. The newspaper reprints materials from other newspapers. I decided to save the newspaper in my town. I didn't go to Kharkiv, where several million people live. I went to a small town called Zinkiv. There are several thousand people in Zinkiv.  The students I studied with called me a fool. The students were on their knees asking to be left to work in Kyiv or other big cities.

          I DID NOT BECOME A TELEVISION REPORTER.

          The gay department controlled the students who studied with me. Some students changed their sexual orientation for the sake of their careers. Students took risks. In the Soviet Union, gays were imprisoned.

          I did not know that my friend was gay. I was sleeping with girls. My gay friend loved me and wanted to commit suicide. I was shocked. Since then we have not been in contact.  My friend happily married an Italian and left Ukraine.

          I DID NOT BECOME GAY.

          I worked hard as a janitor while studying at the university. I worked as a janitor at 3-7 jobs at the same time. I had money for girls. I did not take money from my parents. 

          Students had no money to buy food. Every week my parents sent me food to Kyiv. Many students ate my food. I had many friends.

          I lived in a university dormitory. Many things were stolen from me. They stole everything! Socks, clothes, underwear, shoes, dishes. I didn't have any old things. Because all my things were stolen. I was proud to be stolen from. I bragged to the girls that everything was stolen from me.

          I earned a lot of money. I rented an apartment to meet girls. The girls drugged me with sleeping pills and robbed me.  I could have died. The girls would call me.  We had a lot of fun.

          My uncle in Sevastopol was stealing equipment from submarines. The equipment contained gold, silver, and platinum. My uncle could not get precious metals from radio components. My uncle bequeathed the stolen parts to his children.  My uncle kept the parts at the dacha. Homeless people stole all the parts. The homeless people wrote on the wall of the house thanking their uncle for the non-ferrous metals.

          Many people have many things at home that were stolen at work. My sister and I slept on bedding from a military unit. My father stole 1000 tools at work. My father only needs 10 to 20 tools. As a child, my father was starving. All his life, my father was waiting for war and famine. My parents have 500 cans of canned food at home in case of war or famine. All the canned food is gone.

          I loved my father very much. My father died. All the residents of Zinkiv came to the funeral. I am very grateful. There were many wreaths and flowers on the grave. My father was an angel. My father had no conflicts with anyone.

          I DID NOT BECOME A THIEF.

          I was finishing my studies at the university. I spent 100 days writing my thesis in Moscow.  I wanted to marry a girl living in Moscow. I talked to many Russian girls in Moscow.  I realized that I could only marry a Ukrainian girl.

          In Kharkiv, I helped a girl carry heavy bags. We met and have been together for 40 years.    We have 2 children and 3 grandchildren.

          I DID NOT BECOME A RESIDENT OF MOSCOW.

          I am very similar to my grandfather Petro. He was my mother's father. My grandfather was an alcoholic. After the war, during the famine, my grandfather left his wife and 6 children. My mother and her sisters lived in poverty. My mother and my grandmother went to steal food at night to avoid starvation. My mother's relatives were ashamed of their poverty. They made up stories about their family. They said that our family descended from the Baltic nobility. They lied that during the Second World War, Petro's grandfather was a scout, went behind the front line to follow enemy soldiers, cooperated with the police, was a hypnotist, worked in the government, and was an epileptic. My grandfather used to perform on stage, put a railroad track on his shoulders, with people hanging on the edges of the rail.

          My mother's relatives became liars.

          My grandfather psychologically traumatized his children.

          I am glad that I did not become a single, divorced person.

          My father was proud of his friend, the chief of the secret police in Zinkiv. In a small town, the secret police was unnecessary. There were no spies or traitors in Zinkiv.  The previous chief of the secret police was a drunk and slept on a park bench at night. A friend of my father's was a careerist and kicked out his boss and took his place. The policeman told the content of books and movies about spies, did nothing, drank vodka, asked farmers for food and took the food to his superiors. The policeman became a general. Now he tells us how Ukraine needs to fight Moscow. All his life, the policeman served Moscow well. 

          My father's friend offered me to serve in the secret police. I refused.

          I DID NOT BECOME A SECRET POLICE OFFICER.

          I worked conscientiously in the editorial office of a district newspaper. I wrote a lot of articles for the newspaper. I had 20 pseudonyms. I had many enemies.  I criticized many people. I was protected from revenge by a great leader. When the big boss was about to retire, I quit the newspaper and went to study in Kyiv.

          I DID NOT BECOME A JOURNALIST.

          I studied in Kyiv and got a degree in sociology. There are a lot of lies in sociology. 

          I DID NOT BECOME A SOCIOLOGIST.

          My family really wanted to have an apartment in Kyiv. In Kyiv, I became a janitor at the Art Academy.  After 10 years, they promised to give me an apartment. According to the laws of Ukraine, when a person has worked as a janitor for 10 years and lived in a service apartment, that person should be given.

          Many famous people worked at the Art Academy. The Academy was a center of spirituality. The Academy was a leading artistic educational institution in Ukraine.  But the Academy's leadership, like the rest of the country, gladly plunged into corruption, theft, and fraud. The Academy's management was happy to degrade along with the authorities.    

          Ukraine has corruption like many countries. But in Ukraine, there was a lot of domestic corruption. Ordinary people were forced to bribe the authorities. The presidents who ruled Ukraine before Volodymyr Zelenskyy accustomed Ukrainians to corruption.

          When people refuse to give bribes, they become enemies of the government. The government tries to punish them to intimidate other people into giving bribes. 

          This is what happened to me. I worked as a janitor in Kyiv for 33 years to get an apartment. The authorities wanted me to give a bribe. But I had no money. I did not bribe the authorities. I started writing books about government corruption. The authorities started suing me for my books about corruption.  The authorities want to take away my apartment. The authorities want to scare ordinary people. To stop people from criticizing the authorities. They want people to be afraid of the authorities.

          I DID NOT GET THE APARTMENT IN MY OWNERSHIP.

          My family had no money. My family in Kyiv ate food from Zinkiv. I decided to go into business. Ukrainian business is built on corruption. Entrepreneurs have to bribe the authorities. I didn't have a good accountant. I made accounting mistakes.  The head of the Zinkiv tax office forced me to enroll my daughters in Kyiv University to study law and sociology.

          I was helped by a professor who arranged for me to study journalism.

          I had no money. My wife and I worked at the professor's dacha.

          My relationship with the professor's children deteriorated. The professor's children thought that the professor loved me more than his sons. The professor said that I had fully paid him off. His sons hated me. The children reproached the professor for his friendship with me. I said goodbye to the professor.

          Thank you, professor, for everything. And I wish you all the best.

          I thank the Almighty that the professor became a part of my life. 

          I love the professor from Kyiv very much. The professor is my second father. 

          The head of the Zinkiv tax service took all the bribes for himself. He did not share the bribes with the management. The head was fired from his job. The chief stole a million dollars from an American. The police were looking for the boss. The boss faked his own death and funeral.

          I DID NOT BECOME A BUSINESSMAN.

          My father's friend "Businessman" bought sick cattle from agricultural enterprises and sold meat in big cities.

          "Businessman" often visited my parents' house. My father would invite the head of the district, his deputy, and employees of the state administration. The "businessman" would tell them about his "exploits".

          The authorities promoted a lenient attitude of society towards thieves.  Everyone earns as much as they can! An ordinary person steals $3, and the government steals $3 million.  The law punishes them equally.   I did not condemn the theft of the "businessman".  The "businessman" offered me to sell meat from sick animals in Kyiv markets. The "businessman" offered me to poison customers. I refused.

          "The 'businessman' stole cattle from people. The "businessman" was helped by his two sons. Villagers fired at the family gang. The "businessman" and his sons were injured.

          I DID NOT BECOME A SELLER OF STOLEN GOODS.

          My father's godfather became the head of the Zinkiv district.  My godfather had the same name as my uncle from Deikalivka and a professor from Kyiv. I was very fond of my godfather and called him "uncle" all my life. I considered my uncle from Zinkiv a close and dear person. We were very good friends.

          My uncle's friend was the head of the district. My uncle became his deputy. My uncle fired his friend from his job and took his office. 

          My uncle offered me to work in the district state administration to steal state money. I refused.

          My uncle took a lot of bribes. My uncle was fired from his job. My uncle was afraid to leave the house. Uncle was afraid of the people he robbed.

          Rich children refused to take my uncle to live with them in Kyiv. The uncle paid a bribe and got his son a job at the customs. The son became a general and a big bribe-taker. The uncle was proud that his son robbed people and demanded bribes. The uncle fell ill. His family refused to take care of him.  My uncle was in the hospital. My uncle committed suicide. Uncle took his life support off. At the funeral, my uncle's son said that he approved of his father's decision to die. The residents of Zinkiv were shocked. The uncle had been joking and predicting his death. The uncle made children bad people. Many people suffered from his son.  The uncle suffered from his children.

          It's good that I did not become an official and a corrupt official.

          My wife was born in Western Ukraine. Ternopil region. The village of Zolotnyky.

          Many Ukrainians go to Europe to work. Many residents of Zolotnyky have a lot of money. People do not know what to do with the money. People keep money in bags, like my uncle from Deikalivka. Money in bags is eaten by pests. Banknotes in bags stink. My wife's friends offered to go to Europe. I refused. I have to clean a large park in Kyiv.

          I DID NOT BECOME A GUEST WORKER.

          In 2008, millions of Ukrainians came out to protest against the corrupt government of Ukraine. For several months, 18 people from Western Ukraine lived in my apartment. People came to participate in protests against the Ukrainian government. The government was involved in corruption and theft. People elected a new government. The new government was engaged in corruption and theft.

           In 2014, millions of Ukrainians took part in protests against corrupt officials and thieves. People elected a new government. The new government was engaged in corruption and theft.

          Ukrainians know how to change the government. Ukrainians do not know how to control the government.

          I have written 10 books about corruption in the government of Kyiv and Ukraine.

          Since then, I have had problems with the authorities. The authorities are constantly suing me.

          I held protests against the arbitrariness of the Kyiv authorities.

          Officials threatened to kill me. The rector of the National Academy of Fine Arts and Architecture is the president of the Academy of Arts of Ukraine. The official promised to cut off my head. Ukrainian journalist Georgiy Gongadze, who wrote about corruption, had his head cut off.

          I WAS NOT BEHEADED.

          My close people died during Russia's war against Ukraine. I wrote an anti-war book, Putin's List, about Russian fascists - cultural, educational, media and church figures in Russia.

          I don't want Ukrainian officials to copy Russian officials. 

          I am a romantic and a dreamer. I have remained the same as in my childhood. I have not changed.

          Like in my childhood, I want all people on planet Earth to be happy. And you?

          I HAVE NOT BECOME A BAD PERSON.

          Glory to Ukraine!

          Grigory Leshchenko December 1, 2024
